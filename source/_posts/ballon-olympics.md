<section class="listicle-entry">
  <div>
    <h2>1. A Forgotten Sky‑High Olympic Contest</h2>
    <div class="listicle-meta"><span>1900 Summer Games – Paris</span></div>
    <p>The 1900 Summer Games are best remembered for track‑and‑field spectacles, swimming in the Seine, and even a live pigeon shoot. Yet the official programme also featured **ballooning**. Using gas balloons that required no engines, pilots competed in distance, duration, altitude and precision challenges, turning the sky over the Exposition Universelle into a daring arena.</p>
  </div>
</section>

<section class="listicle-entry">
  <div>
    <h2>2. Why Ballooning Was Considered an Olympic Event</h2>
    <div class="listicle-meta"><span>Four selection criteria</span></div>
    <p>Retrospective analysis of the 1900 Games applies four criteria to decide whether an activity counts as “Olympic”. All four were met by the ballooning contests:</p>
    <ol>
      <li>Restricted to amateurs</li>
      <li>Open to competitors of any nation</li>
      <li>Open to all eligible competitors</li>
      <li>No handicapping system</li>
    </ol>
    <p>Despite satisfying these points, ballooning has generally <strong>not</strong> been classified as an official Olympic discipline in modern records, mainly because the sport faded from later programmes and early documentation was sparse.</p>
  </div>
</section>

<section class="listicle-entry">
  <div>
    <h2>3. The Aeronautical Pioneer Henry de La Vaulx</h2>
    <div class="listicle-meta"><span>World‑record performances</span></div>
    <p>French aristocrat and balloonist <strong>Comte Henry de La Vaulx</strong> dominated the competitions, setting two world records during the Games:</p>
    <ul>
      <li><strong>Distance:</strong> 1 237 km (world record)</li>
      <li><strong>Duration:</strong> 35 h 45 min (world record)</li>
    </ul>
    <p>These feats highlighted the technical skill and endurance required to pilot a gas balloon over long stretches without power.</p>
  </div>
</section>

    <h2>11. Legacy</h2>
    <div class="listicle-meta"><span>Why the sport matters today</span></div>
    <p>Although ballooning never re‑appeared on an Olympic programme, the 1900 events demonstrate how early Games embraced a wide spectrum of physical and technological challenges. The feats of Henry de La Vaulx and his fellow aeronauts remain a remarkable footnote in Olympic history—proof that the sky once served as an Olympic arena.</p>
